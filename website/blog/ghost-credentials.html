The Ghost Credential Crisis.

For twenty years, the enterprise has relied on a lie. We have been told that SSH Keys are the gold standard of infrastructure security. We have built our data centers on the assumption that if a user has the key, they are the user.

We were wrong. SSH keys are Ghost Credentials. They have no identity, no expiry, and no physical anchor. They are copyable strings of text that exist in a vacuum of context.

"An SSH key is a probabilistic guess at identity. Prmana is deterministic evidence."

The Exfiltration Loophole

When a developer’s laptop is compromised, the first thing an attacker looks for is the .ssh directory. Within seconds, your most sensitive credentials are exfiltrated. They are moved to a different machine, in a different country, and used to initiate sessions that your servers will blindly accept.

Why? Because the SSH protocol has no native concept of Proof of Possession. It cannot tell the difference between a key used by a legitimate employee and a key replayed by a malicious actor.

The Prmana Pivot

We built Prmana to end the era of copyable identity. By implementing RFC 9449 (DPoP) at the PAM layer, we bind the OIDC identity to the physical silicon of the machine.

In the Prmana world, a stolen token is a useless token. The evidence of identity is tethered to the hardware. If the hardware isn't present, the access doesn't exist.

It’s time to exorcise the ghosts from your fleet. It’s time for Identity Anchored in Silicon.
